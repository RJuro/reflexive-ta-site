DP-40 — Mary Yankovik Grande

Ellis Island Oral History Project · 12 sections · 406 sentences · coded with 3 lenses

Phillips
Mrs. Grande, could you start by telling me what your name is and where you immigrated from.
Grande
What my maiden name was? My maiden name was, uh, Marija Yankovik.
Phillips
And what do you remember about the journey itself?
Grande
And it was right after World War One, so they stopped it in a lot of places. In London, and I suppose Paris, and all over. But part of our clothes was stolen in Trieste, so a fellow that my mother knew, he went in, I believe, in London, to buy some clothes for us. He came back from the States and who he bought back I don't remember. But, uh, he bought some clothes so we had something to change in to, to wear.
Phillips
And then you went on the ship.
Grande
And we came here, we left there August 12, 1920 and we stayed a few days in Trieste, Italy. And then we got on the ship.
